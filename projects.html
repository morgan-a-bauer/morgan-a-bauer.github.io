Solving Logic Puzzles with Evolutionary Computation

As a final project for CS470 (Evolutionary Computation) at Eckerd College, Ramsay Flower and I designed a solver for logic grid puzzles using evolutionary computing techniques.

We designed a hybrid representation of the puzzle utilizing evolutionary operators for both permutation-based and array-based representations.

To the right, you will see an example of a logic grid puzzle using characters, items, and locations from J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-Earth.

Analytics for Fantasy Basketball

As mentioned on the "About" page, I am a huge basketball fan (especially When it comes to the Sacramento Kings).

I am the co-comissioner of my family's fantasy basketball league. My grandfather often wins the league, so I decided to develop some tools to improve my performance.

These tools include customized draft rankings and a Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) that I use to predict the scores of each player on a daily basis to inform my sit/start decisions.

Upon implementing these tools, I saw an improvement of my win percentage from 47.8% in the 2022-2023 season to 70.3% in the 2023-2024 season. I improved my managerial ranking from 578 (bronze) to 831 (platinum).

Dominion

I am an avid board game enthusiast. While I have played countless games, my favorites are Pendulum and Dominion. As an exercise, my friend, John Cetinkaya, and I decided to implement Dominion.

At the time John was looking to gain some experience designing GUIs, so I agreed to write the game logic. I am currently working on the final details of the game. Once I have completed all aspects of the base game, I will begin to implement expansions.
